Conclusion

The more people you put into the database from the program, the less easy it recognises people. The people that are added will rarely be recognized by the kinect sensor. When they are, it only lasts for a short while (milliseconds). It seems to have a big influence whether a person has long hair, for example girls. The camera doesn't recognise the face as the same one which is stored in the database where the hair isn't in their face.

The position of a person in the 3rd axle (depth axle) seems to a problem too, when a person is a little further away from the mirror than where they stood while teaching the mirror to recognize their face it wont recognize them.

Up until this moment I don't think the face recognition is very reliable unless you make sure the user database contains 3 people or less and the learning template for the mirror needs to make sure the following points are included.

  • Person needs to maintain same spot in front of mirror at all times.
  • No changes of height and depth of the person in front of the mirror.
  • Woman can't have hair in their face (pony tail backwards is sufficient).
  • Only 3 profiles can be created (up to this point of testing).
  • No sudden change of facial hair.

Next step is to set up a 3 picture 3 person profile for the technical part, this will be useful for further testing.
